Song Genre Prediction

Introduction

For our project, we wanted to take data from a set of songs, and then use those songs' features to predict their genre. With the rise of digital streaming in the past decade, accessibility to music has increased drastically and in return so has the demand for people to explore music similar to what they already like. An accurate genre predictor for songs is crucial for music distributing sites such as Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music. Thus, we aim to create an algorithm using K Means and Linear/Logistic Regression to predict which song a genre belongs to.

Data

Our dataset is a collection of 232,725 songs pulled from Spotify's API with a total of 26 different genres and 18 features. We downloaded the dataset from Kaggle. Also, we ended up removing tracks from 11 different genres as we deemed many of the songs in those genres to have been erroneously classified and were detrimental to the overall dataset. Thus, we ended up with 132,097 tracks assigned to 15 genres. The genres included were Country, Dance, Alternative, Electronic, Folk, Hip-Hop, Indie, R&B, Classical, Rap, Raggae, Pop, Rock, and Soul. Out of the 17 other features, we only chose to use 8 as we found the others to either be poor predictors for any genre or inconsequential data like a track's id or URI:

  • Acousticness: Confidence from 0.0 to 1.0 of whether the track is acoustic
  • Danceability: How suitable a track is for dancing from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Energy: How energetic a track is from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Instrumentalness: Predicts whether a track contains no vocals from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Liveness: Detects likelihood of an audience in recording from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Speechiness: Detects presence of spoken words w/o instrumentals from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Valence: Measure from 0.0 to 1.0 of the positivity conveyed by a track
  • Tempo: Overall estimated tempo in beats per minutes (BPM)

Approach/Experiments/Results
