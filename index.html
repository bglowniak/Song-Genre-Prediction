Song Genre Prediction

Introduction

For our project, we wanted to take data from a set of songs, and then use those songs' features to predict their genre. With the rise of digital streaming in the past decade, accessibility to music has increased drastically and in return so has the demand for people to explore music similar to what they already like. An accurate genre predictor for songs is crucial for music distributing sites such as Spotify, Apple Music, and Amazon Music. Thus, we aim to create an algorithm using K Means and Linear/Logistic Regression to predict which song a genre belongs to.

Data

Our dataset is a collection of 232,725 songs pulled from Spotify's API with a total of 26 different genres and 18 features. We downloaded the dataset from Kaggle. Also, we ended up removing tracks from 12 different genres as we deemed many of the songs in those genres to have been erroneously classified and were detrimental to the overall dataset. Thus, we ended up with 132,097 tracks assigned to 14 genres. The genres included were Country, Dance, Alternative, Electronic, Folk, Hip-Hop, Indie, R&B, Classical, Rap, Raggae, Pop, Rock, and Soul. Out of the 17 other features, we only chose to use 10 as we found the others to either be poor predictors for any genre or inconsequential data like a track's id or URI:

  • Key: The key of the track using Pitch Class Notation (0=C, 1=C#, etc)
  • Mode: Mode of the track (0=Minor, 1=Major)
  • Acousticness: Confidence from 0.0 to 1.0 of whether the track is acoustic
  • Danceability: How suitable a track is for dancing from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Energy: How energetic a track is from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Instrumentalness: Predicts whether a track contains no vocals from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Liveness: Detects likelihood of an audience in recording from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Speechiness: Detects presence of spoken words w/o instrumentals from 0.0 to 1.0
  • Valence: Measure from 0.0 to 1.0 of the positivity conveyed by a track
  • Tempo: Overall estimated tempo in beats per minutes (BPM)

Approach/Experiments/Results

For supervised learning, we ran four different algorithms to see if we can predict a track's genre with the following scores:

  • Linear Regression: 0.081
  • Logistic Regression: 0.310
  • Decision Tree: 0.242
  • Random Forest: 0.362
For unsupervised learning, we intended to analyze the performance of each feature in the dataset. First we used Select K Best to score each of the used features:

Conclusion/Future Work

Our outcome was not opitmal as our genre predictions were not consistent. Our dataset did not provide us with the needed information to properly predict genre as our most accurate model was Random Forest with an average accuracy of 36%.

If our methods were refined, we believe that genre classifying would be a useful tool to music lovers everywhere to find new music. It could also be used by those interested in making their own music as a reference for genre baselines in different musical features. To do this, we have come up with a few steps that we can take to improve on our current project.

The potential next steps we would take in furthering this project and obtaining better results would be to be more selctive with what features we are analyzing. Since our current feature analysis was not a consistently good genre predictor, re-evaluating which features are better predictors would be beneficial. This re-evaluation may mean cutting out some features but possibly also considering others that were not originally included in the analysis. In addition to being more selective with features, giving each feature a weight would also help our models better predict genre. This would allow us to still consider multiple features but would put more emphasis on the features that more directly correlate with genre prediction.
